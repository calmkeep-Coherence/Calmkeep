Calmkeep

Runtime Containment for Generative Model Coherence

Why Generative Drift Persists

Modern language models demonstrate remarkable reasoning capacity but frequently exhibit long-form instability across extended generation. Instruction decay, contradiction drift, and context reconstruction overhead remain persistent challenges even in advanced architectures.

Most reliability approaches attempt correction after instability has already emerged — through retrieval augmentation, alignment training, or post-hoc verification. These strategies treat coherence as an evaluative outcome rather than a continuously governed generative property.

Calmkeep explores an alternate hypothesis: that generative coherence may behave as a bounded temporal process that can be stabilized during inference itself.

Bounded-Coherence Runtime Containment

Calmkeep is a model-agnostic middleware framework that operates between prompt conditioning and model output. The system monitors symbolic rhythm across token generation, measuring cadence, density, and phase continuity across successive output windows.

When generation patterns deviate from a bounded coherence envelope, Calmkeep applies adaptive containment modulation designed to preserve semantic continuity while preventing drift escalation.

This approach treats coherence as a dynamic stability constraint rather than a post-generation correctness metric.

Symbolic Rhythm As A Generative Control Dimension

Calmkeep evaluates generative motion using rhythmic signatures derived from:

• Micro-cadence (token emission pacing)
• Symbolic density (conceptual compression per unit)
• Phase continuity (alignment of sequential reasoning flow)

These signals form a composite generative trace that allows containment enforcement to occur during output formation rather than after contradiction emergence.

The framework draws conceptual inspiration from animation continuity principles in which stability emerges through bounded elasticity between sequential frames. Calmkeep extends this idea to symbolic computation by treating tokens as temporal frames within a meaning-continuity waveform.

Runtime Infrastructure Characteristics

Calmkeep is designed to integrate at the API orchestration layer and does not require model retraining or architectural modification. The containment engine operates as an inference-time stability governor compatible with multiple generative model adapters.

Implementation details, envelope thresholds, weighting functions, and enforcement routines remain intentionally abstracted.

Observed Behavioral Effects

Early internal evaluation suggests that runtime containment may reduce emergent contradiction cascades, preserve instruction persistence across extended sessions, and reduce prompt reconstruction overhead during long-form reasoning workflows.

Formal benchmarking and third-party evaluation remain ongoing areas of interest.
